I am a palaeobiologist at the University of Birmingham and my work is broadly focused on how past environmental changes influenced the biodiversity of fossil vertebrates.

My current research, funded by the Leverhulme Trust, focuses on how past climate change impacted the evolution, diversity, and global distribution of dinosaurs and their kin throughout the Mesozoic (251â€“66 million years ago). Underpinning my work is fossil occurrence data from the Paleobiology Database, to which I apply a range of quantitative and phylogenetic methods.

I am also interested in the human factors that influence fossil occurrence data, including how neocolonialism and imperialism impact research practices in palaeontology.
