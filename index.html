I am a (vertebrate) paleobiologist investigating how various environmental, physical, and historical factors drive patterns of deep-time biodiversity. I use a range of statistical and phylogenetic tools alongside fossil occurrence data, environmental data, and bibliometric information to investigate topics such as: (1) the influence of climate on dinosaur evolution; (2) the impact of sampling biases on terrestrial vertebrate (tetrapod) biodiversity through deep-time; and (3) ethical issues and the legacy of colonialism in paleontological research.

Currently, I am based at Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU) in Germany, where I contribute to teaching on the " International Masters in Paleobiology. If you are a perspective student looking to pursue a career in paleobiology, please do get in touch!
